Patrician Brothers' College Fairfield is a Catholic school for boys in Years 7-12, established by the Brothers' of St. Patrick in 1953. Up until now, it has been running for approximately 64 years so far.

The College continues the vision of the Brothers' founder, Bishop Daniel Delany, seeking to recognise and proclaim the presence of Christ in all people and in all of creation as explicitly stated in Saint Patrick's Breastplate Prayer.

The College has recently undergone a building refurbishment program, giving it the modern facilities which we enjoy today such as the recently constructed Technology Building which can be utilized in a myriad of ways relating for the technology subject offered at this college. We aim to provide the very best resources possible for students to learn skills needed to excel.

The College, in its short history has developed a strong reputation in the fields of academic, sporting, debating and other extra-curricular activities - as evident by our fine results such as a good history of students making into Australian and other foreign universities; not to mention our outstanding High Schooler Certificate (HSC) ATAR.

Patrician Brothers' College, Fairfield continues to Strive for Educational Excellence in the Patrician Tradition, providing Quality Catholic Education.
